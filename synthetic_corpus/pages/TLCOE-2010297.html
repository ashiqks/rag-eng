1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Gap Checkout, the current control experience does not sufficiently support aos for the returning visitors segment. Based on prior quality-tactic learnings across the mid tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 28-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Single-page checkout' on Gap Checkout (Tablet Web) to returning visitors, then we expect a lift of at least 0.99% on AOS because a single-page checkout reduces perceived steps and lifts completion rate.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing Checkout experience on Gap Tablet Web.
  • Challenger: Single-page checkout variant — a single-page checkout reduces perceived steps and lifts completion rate.

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 28-day test window, no statistically significant difference was observed on the primary KPI (AOS (Average Order Size)); observed delta 0.21% sits within MDE (0.99%). Net RPV 0.24%, AOS -0.21% — also within noise.
