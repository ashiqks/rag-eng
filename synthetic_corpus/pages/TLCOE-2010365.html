1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Athleta Checkout, the current control experience does not sufficiently support email capture rate for the new visitors segment. Based on prior quality-tactic learnings across the premium tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 180-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Single-page checkout' on Athleta Checkout (Desktop) to new visitors, then we expect a lift of at least 0.93% on Email Capture Rate because a single-page checkout reduces perceived steps and lifts completion rate.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing Checkout experience on Athleta Desktop.
  • Challenger: Single-page checkout variant — a single-page checkout reduces perceived steps and lifts completion rate.

2. Test Results

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 180-day test window, no statistically significant difference was observed on the primary KPI (Email Capture Rate); observed delta -0.04% sits within MDE (0.93%). Net RPV -0.13%, AOS -0.07% — also within noise.
