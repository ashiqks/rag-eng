1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Gap PDP, the current control experience does not sufficiently support checkout completion rate for the returning visitors segment. Based on prior quality-tactic learnings across the mid tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 90-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'PDP module reorder' on Gap PDP (Desktop) to returning visitors, then we expect a lift of at least 1.14% on Checkout Completion Rate because moving 'you may also like' above 'recently viewed' on pdp exposes more new products and lifts cross-sell.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing PDP experience on Gap Desktop.
  • Challenger: PDP module reorder variant — moving 'you may also like' above 'recently viewed' on pdp exposes more new products and lifts cross-sell.

2. Test Results

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 90-day test window, no statistically significant difference was observed on the primary KPI (Checkout Completion Rate); observed delta 0.07% sits within MDE (1.14%). Net RPV 0.14%, AOS -0.26% — also within noise.
