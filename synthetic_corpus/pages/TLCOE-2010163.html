1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Gap PDP, the current control experience does not sufficiently support email capture rate for the non-loyalty segment. Based on prior quality-tactic learnings across the mid tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 60-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'PDP module reorder' on Gap PDP (Desktop) to non-loyalty, then we expect a lift of at least 1.2% on Email Capture Rate because moving 'you may also like' above 'recently viewed' on pdp exposes more new products and lifts cross-sell.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing PDP experience on Gap Desktop.
  • Challenger: PDP module reorder variant — moving 'you may also like' above 'recently viewed' on pdp exposes more new products and lifts cross-sell.

2. Test Results

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 60-day test window, the challenger outperformed the control on the primary KPI (Email Capture Rate) by 0.81% (statistically significant at the configured threshold). Net RPV moved by 0.84% and AOS by 1.11%.
