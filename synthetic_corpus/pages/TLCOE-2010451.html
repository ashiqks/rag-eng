1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Old Navy Checkout, the current control experience does not sufficiently support checkout completion rate for the loyalty members segment. Based on prior urgency-tactic learnings across the discount tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 150-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Checkout NDD countdown' on Old Navy Checkout (Tablet Web) to loyalty members, then we expect a lift of at least 1.35% on Checkout Completion Rate because a countdown to the next-day delivery cutoff drives urgency at checkout.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing Checkout experience on Old Navy Tablet Web.
  • Challenger: Checkout NDD countdown variant — a countdown to the next-day delivery cutoff drives urgency at checkout.

2. Test Results

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 150-day test window, the challenger underperformed the control on the primary KPI (Checkout Completion Rate) by 2.3% (statistically significant in the wrong direction). Net RPV moved by -1.92% and AOS by -1.08%.

2.2 Variation Description

  • Control: as-is Checkout on Old Navy Tablet Web.
  • Challenger: applies the Checkout NDD countdown treatment described in §1.3.
