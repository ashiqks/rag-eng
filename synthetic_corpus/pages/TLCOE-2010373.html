1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Old Navy Account, the current control experience does not sufficiently support pdp -> shopping bag conversion for the new visitors segment. Based on prior value-tactic learnings across the discount tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 90-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Loyalty progress widget' on Old Navy Account (Android Native) to new visitors, then we expect a lift of at least 0.83% on PDP -> Shopping Bag Conversion because a progress widget toward the next loyalty tier raises return-visit rate among members.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing Account experience on Old Navy Android Native.
  • Challenger: Loyalty progress widget variant — a progress widget toward the next loyalty tier raises return-visit rate among members.

2. Test Results

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 90-day test window, no statistically significant difference was observed on the primary KPI (PDP -> Shopping Bag Conversion); observed delta -0.12% sits within MDE (0.83%). Net RPV 0.16%, AOS -0.16% — also within noise.
