1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Banana Republic Checkout, the current control experience does not sufficiently support aur for the first-time buyers segment. Based on prior quality-tactic learnings across the premium tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 42-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Single-page checkout' on Banana Republic Checkout (Desktop) to first-time buyers, then we expect a lift of at least 0.52% on AUR because a single-page checkout reduces perceived steps and lifts completion rate.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing Checkout experience on Banana Republic Desktop.
  • Challenger: Single-page checkout variant — a single-page checkout reduces perceived steps and lifts completion rate.

2. Test Results

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 42-day test window, no statistically significant difference was observed on the primary KPI (AUR (Average Unit Retail)); observed delta 0.14% sits within MDE (0.52%). Net RPV 0.12%, AOS -0.18% — also within noise.
