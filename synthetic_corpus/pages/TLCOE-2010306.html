1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Gap Factory PLP, the current control experience does not sufficiently support checkout completion rate for the loyalty members segment. Based on prior quality-tactic learnings across the discount tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 60-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'PLP grid density' on Gap Factory PLP (Tablet Web) to loyalty members, then we expect a lift of at least 1.01% on Checkout Completion Rate because increasing product density on plp improves visibility of more skus and lifts add-to-bag.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing PLP experience on Gap Factory Tablet Web.
  • Challenger: PLP grid density variant — increasing product density on plp improves visibility of more skus and lifts add-to-bag.

2. Test Results

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 60-day test window, the challenger outperformed the control on the primary KPI (Checkout Completion Rate) by 2.27% (statistically significant at the configured threshold). Net RPV moved by 1.48% and AOS by 1.41%.
