1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Gap Shopping Bag, the current control experience does not sufficiently support add-to-bag rate for the all customers segment. Based on prior time savings-tactic learnings across the mid tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 120-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Bag auto promo' on Gap Shopping Bag (Desktop) to all customers, then we expect a lift of at least 1.04% on Add-to-Bag Rate because automatically applying the best eligible promo code lifts conversion in the bag step.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing Shopping Bag experience on Gap Desktop.
  • Challenger: Bag auto promo variant — automatically applying the best eligible promo code lifts conversion in the bag step.

2. Test Results

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 120-day test window, no statistically significant difference was observed on the primary KPI (Add-to-Bag Rate); observed delta 0.11% sits within MDE (1.04%). Net RPV -0.03%, AOS -0.3% — also within noise.

2.2 Variation Description

  • Control: as-is Shopping Bag on Gap Desktop.
  • Challenger: applies the Bag auto promo treatment described in §1.3.
  • Screenshots: see Confluence attachments control_T-GAP-00336.png, challenger_T-GAP-00336.png (omitted from this synthetic export).

2.3 Details

Across the 120-day test window, no statistically significant difference was observed on the primary KPI (Add-to-Bag Rate); observed delta 0.11% sits within MDE (1.04%). Net RPV -0.03%, AOS -0.3% — also within noise.
