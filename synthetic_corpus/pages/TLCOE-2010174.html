1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Gap Factory Checkout, the current control experience does not sufficiently support aur for the first-time buyers segment. Based on prior time savings-tactic learnings across the discount tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 150-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Guest checkout default' on Gap Factory Checkout (iOS Native) to first-time buyers, then we expect a lift of at least 1.32% on AUR because defaulting to guest checkout (with optional signup at the end) lifts checkout completion.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing Checkout experience on Gap Factory iOS Native.
  • Challenger: Guest checkout default variant — defaulting to guest checkout (with optional signup at the end) lifts checkout completion.

2. Test Results

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 150-day test window, the challenger outperformed the control on the primary KPI (AUR (Average Unit Retail)) by 5.32% (statistically significant at the configured threshold). Net RPV moved by 6.88% and AOS by 0.22%.
