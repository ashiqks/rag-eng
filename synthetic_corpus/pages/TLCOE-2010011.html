1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Banana Republic Factory Shopping Bag, the current control experience does not sufficiently support checkout completion rate for the new visitors segment. Based on prior quality-tactic learnings across the discount tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 42-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Bag inline gift card' on Banana Republic Factory Shopping Bag (iOS Native) to new visitors, then we expect a lift of at least 1.14% on Checkout Completion Rate because allowing gift-card entry inline in the bag (vs. checkout) reduces drop-off.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing Shopping Bag experience on Banana Republic Factory iOS Native.
  • Challenger: Bag inline gift card variant — allowing gift-card entry inline in the bag (vs. checkout) reduces drop-off.

Across the 42-day test window, the challenger outperformed the control on the primary KPI (Checkout Completion Rate) by 2.68% (statistically significant at the configured threshold). Net RPV moved by 3.63% and AOS by -0.23%.
