1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Banana Republic Factory Shopping Bag, the current control experience does not sufficiently support checkout completion rate for the all customers segment. Based on prior value-tactic learnings across the discount tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 150-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Bag thermometer visibility' on Banana Republic Factory Shopping Bag (Mobile Web) to all customers, then we expect a lift of at least 1.15% on Checkout Completion Rate because a persistent thermometer educates the shopper on the free-ship benefit and raises aos.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing Shopping Bag experience on Banana Republic Factory Mobile Web.
  • Challenger: Bag thermometer visibility variant — a persistent thermometer educates the shopper on the free-ship benefit and raises aos.

Across the 150-day test window, the challenger underperformed the control on the primary KPI (Checkout Completion Rate) by 0.44% (statistically significant in the wrong direction). Net RPV moved by -0.49% and AOS by -0.52%.
