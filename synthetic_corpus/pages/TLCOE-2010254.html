1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Old Navy Checkout, the current control experience does not sufficiently support email capture rate for the new visitors segment. Based on prior urgency-tactic learnings across the discount tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 60-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Checkout NDD countdown' on Old Navy Checkout (iOS Native) to new visitors, then we expect a lift of at least 1.32% on Email Capture Rate because a countdown to the next-day delivery cutoff drives urgency at checkout.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing Checkout experience on Old Navy iOS Native.
  • Challenger: Checkout NDD countdown variant — a countdown to the next-day delivery cutoff drives urgency at checkout.

2. Test Results

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 60-day test window, the challenger underperformed the control on the primary KPI (Email Capture Rate) by 2.96% (statistically significant in the wrong direction). Net RPV moved by -3.25% and AOS by -2.24%.
