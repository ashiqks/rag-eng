1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Banana Republic Shopping Bag, the current control experience does not sufficiently support upt for the first-time buyers segment. Based on prior quality-tactic learnings across the premium tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 14-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Bag payment collapse' on Banana Republic Shopping Bag (Mobile Web) to first-time buyers, then we expect a lift of at least 1.25% on UPT because collapsing the alternative payment options on ios bag reduces visual clutter and lifts checkout-start.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing Shopping Bag experience on Banana Republic Mobile Web.
  • Challenger: Bag payment collapse variant — collapsing the alternative payment options on ios bag reduces visual clutter and lifts checkout-start.

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 14-day test window, no statistically significant difference was observed on the primary KPI (UPT (Units Per Transaction)); observed delta -0.25% sits within MDE (1.25%). Net RPV -0.17%, AOS 0.21% — also within noise.
