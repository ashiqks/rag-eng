1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Gap Shopping Bag, the current control experience does not sufficiently support checkout completion rate for the first-time buyers segment. Based on prior quality-tactic learnings across the mid tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 42-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Bag payment collapse' on Gap Shopping Bag (Android Native) to first-time buyers, then we expect a lift of at least 0.53% on Checkout Completion Rate because collapsing the alternative payment options on ios bag reduces visual clutter and lifts checkout-start.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing Shopping Bag experience on Gap Android Native.
  • Challenger: Bag payment collapse variant — collapsing the alternative payment options on ios bag reduces visual clutter and lifts checkout-start.

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 42-day test window, no statistically significant difference was observed on the primary KPI (Checkout Completion Rate); observed delta 0.04% sits within MDE (0.53%). Net RPV -0.3%, AOS -0.23% — also within noise.
