1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Banana Republic PLP, the current control experience does not sufficiently support checkout start rate for the high-aov customers segment. Based on prior quality-tactic learnings across the premium tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 90-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'PLP grid density' on Banana Republic PLP (iOS Native) to high-aov customers, then we expect a lift of at least 1.29% on Checkout Start Rate because increasing product density on plp improves visibility of more skus and lifts add-to-bag.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing PLP experience on Banana Republic iOS Native.
  • Challenger: PLP grid density variant — increasing product density on plp improves visibility of more skus and lifts add-to-bag.

2. Test Results

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 90-day test window, the challenger underperformed the control on the primary KPI (Checkout Start Rate) by 3.62% (statistically significant in the wrong direction). Net RPV moved by -3.74% and AOS by -2.1%.
