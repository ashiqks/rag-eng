1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Gap Checkout, the current control experience does not sufficiently support pdp -> shopping bag conversion for the loyalty members segment. Based on prior quality-tactic learnings across the mid tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 90-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Sticky checkout CTA' on Gap Checkout (Desktop) to loyalty members, then we expect a lift of at least 0.59% on PDP -> Shopping Bag Conversion because a sticky bottom cta on mobile checkout reduces drop-off at the payment step.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing Checkout experience on Gap Desktop.
  • Challenger: Sticky checkout CTA variant — a sticky bottom cta on mobile checkout reduces drop-off at the payment step.

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 90-day test window, no statistically significant difference was observed on the primary KPI (PDP -> Shopping Bag Conversion); observed delta 0.26% sits within MDE (0.59%). Net RPV 0.06%, AOS 0.03% — also within noise.
