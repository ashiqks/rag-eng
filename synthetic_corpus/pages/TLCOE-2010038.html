1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Banana Republic Checkout, the current control experience does not sufficiently support aur for the non-loyalty segment. Based on prior quality-tactic learnings across the premium tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 90-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Sticky checkout CTA' on Banana Republic Checkout (Loyalty Email) to non-loyalty, then we expect a lift of at least 1.01% on AUR because a sticky bottom cta on mobile checkout reduces drop-off at the payment step.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing Checkout experience on Banana Republic Loyalty Email.
  • Challenger: Sticky checkout CTA variant — a sticky bottom cta on mobile checkout reduces drop-off at the payment step.

2. Test Results

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 90-day test window, the challenger underperformed the control on the primary KPI (AUR (Average Unit Retail)) by 3.22% (statistically significant in the wrong direction). Net RPV moved by -2.14% and AOS by -2.45%.
