1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Old Navy Shopping Bag, the current control experience does not sufficiently support checkout start rate for the first-time buyers segment. Based on prior value-tactic learnings across the discount tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 60-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Bag thermometer visibility' on Old Navy Shopping Bag (Desktop) to first-time buyers, then we expect a lift of at least 1.42% on Checkout Start Rate because a persistent thermometer educates the shopper on the free-ship benefit and raises aos.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing Shopping Bag experience on Old Navy Desktop.
  • Challenger: Bag thermometer visibility variant — a persistent thermometer educates the shopper on the free-ship benefit and raises aos.

2. Test Results

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 60-day test window, the challenger underperformed the control on the primary KPI (Checkout Start Rate) by 2.99% (statistically significant in the wrong direction). Net RPV moved by -2.67% and AOS by -1.42%.
