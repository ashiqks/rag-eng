1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Gap PDP, the current control experience does not sufficiently support checkout completion rate for the returning visitors segment. Based on prior urgency-tactic learnings across the mid tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 60-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'PDP scarcity label' on Gap PDP (Tablet Web) to returning visitors, then we expect a lift of at least 0.84% on Checkout Completion Rate because a scarcity label on pdp triggers urgency and lifts atb rate.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing PDP experience on Gap Tablet Web.
  • Challenger: PDP scarcity label variant — a scarcity label on pdp triggers urgency and lifts atb rate.

2. Test Results

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 60-day test window, the challenger outperformed the control on the primary KPI (Checkout Completion Rate) by 3.63% (statistically significant at the configured threshold). Net RPV moved by 2.66% and AOS by -0.24%.

2.2 Variation Description

  • Control: as-is PDP on Gap Tablet Web.
  • Challenger: applies the PDP scarcity label treatment described in §1.3.
