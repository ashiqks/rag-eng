1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Athleta PLP, the current control experience does not sufficiently support pdp -> shopping bag conversion for the cart abandoners segment. Based on prior time savings-tactic learnings across the premium tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 180-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Sticky PLP filters' on Athleta PLP (Android Native) to cart abandoners, then we expect a lift of at least 0.65% on PDP -> Shopping Bag Conversion because a persistent filter rail removes the need to scroll back to the top, accelerating refinement and conversion.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing PLP experience on Athleta Android Native.
  • Challenger: Sticky PLP filters variant — a persistent filter rail removes the need to scroll back to the top, accelerating refinement and conversion.

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 180-day test window, no statistically significant difference was observed on the primary KPI (PDP -> Shopping Bag Conversion); observed delta -0.18% sits within MDE (0.65%). Net RPV 0.2%, AOS -0.01% — also within noise.
