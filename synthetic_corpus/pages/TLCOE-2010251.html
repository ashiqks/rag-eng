1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Gap PLP, the current control experience does not sufficiently support net rpv for the returning visitors segment. Based on prior time savings-tactic learnings across the mid tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 28-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Sticky PLP filters' on Gap PLP (Android Native) to returning visitors, then we expect a lift of at least 0.95% on Net RPV because a persistent filter rail removes the need to scroll back to the top, accelerating refinement and conversion.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing PLP experience on Gap Android Native.
  • Challenger: Sticky PLP filters variant — a persistent filter rail removes the need to scroll back to the top, accelerating refinement and conversion.

2. Test Results

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 28-day test window, the challenger underperformed the control on the primary KPI (Net RPV) by 3.94% (statistically significant in the wrong direction). Net RPV moved by -4.31% and AOS by -0.34%.
