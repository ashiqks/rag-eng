1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Gap Checkout, the current control experience does not sufficiently support upt for the returning visitors segment. Based on prior time savings-tactic learnings across the mid tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 28-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Wallet pay promotion' on Gap Checkout (iOS Native) to returning visitors, then we expect a lift of at least 0.86% on UPT because promoting wallet pay above the billing form shortens checkout for mobile users.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing Checkout experience on Gap iOS Native.
  • Challenger: Wallet pay promotion variant — promoting wallet pay above the billing form shortens checkout for mobile users.

2. Test Results

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 28-day test window, the challenger outperformed the control on the primary KPI (UPT (Units Per Transaction)) by 2.76% (statistically significant at the configured threshold). Net RPV moved by 2.87% and AOS by 0.34%.
