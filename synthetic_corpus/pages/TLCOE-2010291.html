1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Gap Factory Shopping Bag, the current control experience does not sufficiently support opv conversion rate for the loyalty members segment. Based on prior quality-tactic learnings across the discount tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 60-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Bag payment collapse' on Gap Factory Shopping Bag (Tablet Web) to loyalty members, then we expect a lift of at least 0.61% on OPV Conversion Rate because collapsing the alternative payment options on ios bag reduces visual clutter and lifts checkout-start.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing Shopping Bag experience on Gap Factory Tablet Web.
  • Challenger: Bag payment collapse variant — collapsing the alternative payment options on ios bag reduces visual clutter and lifts checkout-start.

2. Test Results

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 60-day test window, the challenger underperformed the control on the primary KPI (OPV Conversion Rate) by 1.98% (statistically significant in the wrong direction). Net RPV moved by -2.32% and AOS by -0.34%.
