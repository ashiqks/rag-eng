1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Old Navy Checkout, the current control experience does not sufficiently support email capture rate for the cart abandoners segment. Based on prior quality-tactic learnings across the discount tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 150-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'Single-page checkout' on Old Navy Checkout (Loyalty Email) to cart abandoners, then we expect a lift of at least 1.01% on Email Capture Rate because a single-page checkout reduces perceived steps and lifts completion rate.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing Checkout experience on Old Navy Loyalty Email.
  • Challenger: Single-page checkout variant — a single-page checkout reduces perceived steps and lifts completion rate.

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 150-day test window, no statistically significant difference was observed on the primary KPI (Email Capture Rate); observed delta 0.04% sits within MDE (1.01%). Net RPV -0.19%, AOS 0.24% — also within noise.
