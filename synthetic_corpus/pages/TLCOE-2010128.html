1. Test Plan

1.1 Problem Statement

On Old Navy PDP, the current control experience does not sufficiently support upt for the first-time buyers segment. Based on prior urgency-tactic learnings across the discount tier, we want to evaluate whether the proposed change moves the needle within the 60-day window.

1.2 Hypothesis

If we apply 'PDP scarcity label' on Old Navy PDP (Tablet Web) to first-time buyers, then we expect a lift of at least 1.45% on UPT because a scarcity label on pdp triggers urgency and lifts atb rate.

1.3 Experimental Changes

  • Control: existing PDP experience on Old Navy Tablet Web.
  • Challenger: PDP scarcity label variant — a scarcity label on pdp triggers urgency and lifts atb rate.

2. Test Results

2.1 Findings (TL;DR)

Across the 60-day test window, the challenger underperformed the control on the primary KPI (UPT (Units Per Transaction)) by 2.59% (statistically significant in the wrong direction). Net RPV moved by -2.84% and AOS by -0.51%.

2.2 Variation Description
